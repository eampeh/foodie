Food is categorized into 3 main groups; namely Body Building, Energy Giving and Protective Foods.


Energy-Giving Foods

Energy-giving foods are include as Carbohydrates and Fats and oils. They are those that supply the body with energy to accomplish work after getting digested. They have a relatively higher sugar content than the other kinds of food. Carbohydrates are one of the primary sources of energy for our bodies. Bread, grains, and starchy foods are some examples of food that are rich in carbohydrates. Fats refer to any ester of fatty acids, or a mixture of similar compounds, most typically found in living creatures or food. Cheese, nuts, and butter are examples of food rich in fat. They are rich in omega-3 fatty acids.


Body-Building Foods

Body-building foods are also classified as proteins. They are rich in vitamins A, B, C, D, and K.They help us to grow, build up our bones and muscles, and also to grow strong and healthy. In order to build lean muscle, you need high protein foods lean in fats, like fish, eggs, chicken breast, and 90% fat-free turkey patties. Also, beans (Black, red, chickpeas) contain proteins to a lesser degree. Don't forget dairy group products such as yogurt, milk, and cheese are also included.


Protective Foods

These are foods that protect our human system. They contain antioxidants that help the body fight against diseases and foreign materials. Protective foods contain mainly fruits and vegetables. Oranges, lettuce, coconut, banana, carrots, and garlic are all examples of protective foods.
